Academic discussions: encouraging our students to speak with confidence and clarity.
Speaking your mind, minding how you speak: encouraging our students to speak with confidence and clarity in academic discussions

While developing speaking skills is a common priority for our students, we may fail to specifically focus on this in class. Instead, we provide the grammar and lexis instruction and hope the spoken language will fall into place as a result. This might be the case, but often learners require specific coaching in their speaking skills. Academic discussion is one area in particular where specific guidance is required to make our students articulate and fluent communicators. In this session, we will investigate techniques that will improve your learners’ ability to present their ideas clearly and effectively in informed debates both during their time at university and beyond in their professional careers.

Avoiding the plateau: developing an effective approach to teaching C1 and higher level students

In order to maintain the motivation of our C1 and higher level students we need to develop an approach which will deal with their complex needs effectively and enable them to progress on their journey towards attaining native-like use of the language. In this webinar we will discuss the numerous challenges which are inherent in teaching advanced level students and look at activities and techniques to help them ‘avoid the plateau’ and enhance their ability to work independently outside of class.
